In AddEmp table, the roles start from number 1 to 5.

The following is an explanation of access levels.

Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) Matrix
Level 1: Chief Executive Officer (CEO)
Role Title: Super Administrator

Permissions:

Holds full and absolute authority across all administrative and technical aspects of the system with zero restrictions.

Level 2: Deputy CEO / General Manager
Role Title: Advanced Administrator

Permissions:

Holds all operational and administrative privileges across the system.

Sole Restriction: Not authorized to permanently delete employee accounts (to ensure data integrity).

Level 3: Department Head
Role Title: Department Manager

Permissions:

Task Management: Can assign tasks directly to employees within their own department. Cross-departmental task assignments strictly require prior approval from the respective Department Head.

Account Management: Authorized to suspend or deactivate accounts of employees within their department only.

Data & Analytics: Access to dashboards and reports is strictly restricted to their own department's analytics.

Access Promotion: Authorized to elevate an employee's access level up to Level 3 maximum.

Level 4: Sub-Department Supervisor
Role Title: Operations Supervisor

Permissions:

Data & Analytics: Granted View-Only access to the analytics and reports of their specific sub-department.

Task Management: Authorized to create and assign tasks to employees within their sub-department.

Restrictions: No authority to delete, deactivate, or add employees. User provisioning (adding employees) must be requested through the Department Head (Level 3) or higher management.

Level 5: Executive Employee
Role Title: End User

Permissions:

Task Reception: Role is limited to receiving, executing, and updating the status of assigned tasks.

Holds no administrative privileges, cannot modify system configurations, and has no access to departmental analytics.
